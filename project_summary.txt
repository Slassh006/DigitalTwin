======================================================
PROJECT ENDOTWIN: COMPREHENSIVE ANALYSIS
======================================================

1. WHAT IS IT FOR?
EndoTwin is a next-generation Medical Digital Twin platform designed to predict, visualize, and analyze endometriosis risk. By fusing distributed patient intelligence through Federated Learning with the physical realism of Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs), it offers doctors and researchers a robust, privacy-first diagnostic tool that translates clinical data into a realistic 3D holographic simulation of a patient's reproductive health.

2. HOW IT WORKS (CORE FUNCTIONS & ARCHITECTURE)
* Federated Learning Ecosystem: The platform operates on distributed nodes (Imaging, Clinical, Pathology) where raw patient data stays localized. A central server aggregates the localized learned patterns to update a global predictive model without ever moving sensitive health records.
* Physics-Informed Neural Network (PINN): Hosted by a FastAPI and PyTorch central server backend, the AI doesn't just "guess" numbers; it relies on mathematical biomechanics. Real tissue elasticity (Lamé parameters) is built firmly into the AI's loss function to enforce biological bounds—making predictions medically rigorous.
* The EndoTwin Console: A Next.js 14 frontend utilizing VTK.js to provide real-time 3D volume rendering, streaming training logs, and longitudinal patient health analytics.

3. HOW WE TRAIN WITH THE DATASET
The project uses a custom PyTorch data pipeline (`data_loader.py`) to systematically train on actual patient records rather than mock numbers. It harmonizes multi-modal data:
* Clinical Data: Age, BMI, pain scores (dysmenorrhea).
* Pathology Data: Blood/lab indicators such as WBC and specific biomarkers (CA-125, CA-19_9).
* Imaging Features: Detailed numerical arrays decoded from scan analyses.
* Ground Truth Labels: A dataset dictating the presence of endometriosis and corresponding physical stiffness of the tissue (in kilopascals, or kPa).
Training occurs in a distributed loop where the `PINNLoss` combines standard AI prediction data loss with a physics consistency loss, guaranteeing the network learns that abnormal features map strictly to stiffer biological tissues (clamped to realistic 0-10 kPa bounds).

4. HOW WE IMPLEMENTED THE DIGITAL TWIN UTERUS
The 3D "Digital Twin" converts abstract AI predictions into tangible, interactive clinical anatomy:
* Rendering via VTK.js: An anatomical blueprint (`generateDefaultVolume`) is rendered in the web browser space complete with proper medical imaging transfer functions utilizing `vtkColorTransferFunction`.
* Dynamic Stiffness Heatmap: As the PINN backend generates predictions via `/predict`, it computes a unique tissue stiffness tensor. The frontend visually layers this tensor using VTK.js piecewise opacity thresholds:
    - Green Surface (< 2 kPa): Healthy, elastic tissue.
    - Yellow Surface (2-5 kPa): Moderate tissue stiffening or scarring.
    - Red Surface (> 5 kPa): Highly stiffened area likely possessing an endometriotic lesion.
* Data Overlays & Lesion Callouts: By linking frontend hovering callbacks to the model's computed parameters, doctors can hover their mouse directly over the 3D organ to scan its real-time kPa readings. Distinct Lesion Markers with analytical severity metrics ("Low", "Moderate", "High" Risk Score) emerge naturally where the AI identifies high stiffness. 
* Hologram Animations: The organ lives inside a `RotatingGroup` component accompanied by grid effects and holographic particles, unifying all spatial data into one synchronized visual representation.
